Joseph Stalin

Early Life

Joseph Stalin was born on December 21, 1879. He was born into poverty in a town named Gori, Georgia.
His dad was a cobbler and he supported the family. Stalin graduated at the top of his class in a little school in Gori.
He got a scholarship to Tiflis Theological Seminary. It was here that he was shown Marxism. Marxism is a political theory
that was the precursor to Communism. Stalin eventually stopped studying and went to meetings with a Marxist group.
He was expelled from Tiflis Theological Seminary in 1899.

Criminal Life

After Stalin was expelled from Tiflis he clased with the government and was exiled to Siberia. He escaped and joined the
Bolshevik Party and was one of their propagandists. After the Russian Revolution he was awarded the job of general secretary
of the Central Comittee of the CPSU.

Rise to Power

Stalin's jon as general secratary of the Central Commitee of the CPSU ended up getting him were he wanted to be: dictator.
On January 21, 1924 Vladimir Lenin died. I became a joint leader of the USSR along with Lev Kamenev and Grigory Zinoviev.
But after awhile, Kamenev lost is spot in the party and Zinoviev was accused by Stalin for using the party for things other than
politics. Finall, Stalin became the uncontested dictator.

Achievements While in Power

While Stalin was in power he achieved many things. For example, Stalin, using the five year plan, industrialized the USSR.
He pushed back a Nazi invasion and was on the winning side of World War Two. After World War Two, Stalin expanded the
USSR's atomic bomb program and in August 29, 1949 the USSR blew up their first atomic bomb. Finally, Stalin started the
spread of communism through the Korean War.
